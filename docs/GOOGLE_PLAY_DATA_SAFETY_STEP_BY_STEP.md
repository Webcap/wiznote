# Google Play Data Safety - Step-by-Step Visual Guide

## 🎯 Goal: Fix Your App Rejection

Your app was rejected because Google detected data transmission that wasn't declared. This guide will walk you through EXACTLY what to click and select.

---

## 📍 WHERE TO GO

1. Open: **https://play.google.com/console**
2. Select: **WizNote** app
3. Click: **Policy** (left sidebar)
4. Click: **App content**
5. Find: **Data safety** section
6. Click: **Start** or **Manage**

## 🚨 STILL GETTING REJECTED?

If you still get rejected after following this guide:

### Check the Rejection Email Carefully

Google will tell you:
- What data they detected
- What's missing from your declaration

### Common Additional Issues:

1. **Google Play SDK Index**
   - Go to: https://play.google.com/sdks
   - Search for "Stripe"
   - Ensure you match their guidance

2. **Privacy Policy Link**
   - Ensure your privacy policy URL is accessible
   - Must be publicly accessible (no login required)

3. **Version Mismatch**
   - Ensure you're declaring data for the CURRENT version
   - If you updated your app, update the Data Safety form

## 🎓 KEY LESSONS

1. **Stripe SDK = Device IDs**
   - Stripe ALWAYS collects device identifiers for fraud prevention
   - You MUST declare this even if you don't explicitly request it

2. **Shared vs Collected**
   - "Collected" = Your app gathers it
   - "Shared" = Sent to third parties (Stripe, Supabase)
   - Payment data is BOTH

3. **Required vs Optional**
   - If users can't use your app without it = Required
   - If only Premium users need it = Optional (payment features)

4. **Ephemeral Processing**
   - Only select "Yes" if data is NEVER stored anywhere
   - Since you store everything in Supabase = always "No"
